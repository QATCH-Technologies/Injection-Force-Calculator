About This Model

This tool is an open injection-force screening model that fits a viscosity-shear-rate profile to the Carreau-Yasuda model and estimates plunger force using a hydrodynamic framework.

Allmendinger et al., Rheological characterization and injection forces of concentrated protein formulations: An alternative predictive model for non-Newtonian solutions, Eur. J. Pharm. Biopharm. (2014). doi:10.1016/j.ejpb.2014.01.009

It should be considered an approximate educational / early-screening implementation, not the original published model.

Two-step workflow
  • Step 1 - Viscosity profile. Pick Newtonian (one viscosity, n = 1) or Custom fit (≥ 4 shear-rate / viscosity points, or a CSV). Click Fit to get η0, η∞, n and γc plus the curve.
  • Step 2 - Syringe & injection. Enter barrel radius, needle dimensions and an injection rate. The force calculation needs the viscosity profile, so running it fits the profile if you have not yet.
Uncertainty bands

When shear-rate coverage is limited, an ensemble of plausible fits that still agree with the data is generated, so the confidence band widens naturally in unmeasured (e.g. high-shear needle) regions.

How force is estimated
  • Effective needle shear rate γeff= (2Q / πRn3) · (3n+1)/(2n+1)
  • Viscosity at γeff read from the fitted profile (with its band)
  • Hydrodynamic force from Hagen-Poiseuille: F = 8 η L Q Rb2 / Rn4
  • Stopper / barrel friction added as an offset term
Empirical correction φ

Real prefilled syringes and staked needles are not ideal flow channels. An optional shape / needle-tip factor φ (Chen-Wei / WuXi approach) scales the hydrodynamic term to improve agreement with measured force.
